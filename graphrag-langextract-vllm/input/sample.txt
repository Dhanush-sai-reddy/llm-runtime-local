The Quasar Corporation was founded in 1985 by Dr. Elara Vance. Dr. Vance, a former researcher at the Global Institute of Technology (GIT), envisioned a company that could harness the power of quantum computing for everyday applications. Quasar Corporation is headquartered in Neo-Veridian, a bustling metropolis known for its technological advancements.

In 2010, Quasar Corporation acquired its main rival, Zenith Dynamics. Zenith Dynamics was led by Marcus Thorne, who subsequently joined Quasar's board of directors. The acquisition allowed Quasar to integrate Zenith's proprietary "Quantum Core" technology into their flagship product, the "Q-Sphere." 

The Q-Sphere project was managed by Sarah Jenkins, a brilliant engineer who had previously worked with Dr. Vance at GIT. Sarah Jenkins collaborated closely with the Delta Research Group, an independent laboratory that provided the specialized materials needed for the Q-Sphere's construction. The Q-Sphere revolutionized the energy sector, providing clean, limitless power to Neo-Veridian and beyond.

However, in 2022, a controversy arose when an investigative journalist, David Chen, published an article claiming that the Delta Research Group's material sourcing practices were unethical. The article sparked a major investigation by the Global Regulatory Commission (GRC). The GRC's lead investigator, Inspector Aris Thorne (no relation to Marcus Thorne), found no conclusive evidence of wrongdoing, but the scandal temporarily impacted Quasar Corporation's stock price.
